# Reviewer Guide — how to review a PhysicsMind simulation

Audience: a physics / math / chemistry teacher reviewing a simulation. The founder hands or explains
this. It's the same for every reviewer (reusable as we add reviewers 2–5).

## The one big idea

**You are the expert. The simulation is a visual aid you point at while *you* teach — not a script you
read from.** Teach the way you always teach. If you ever find yourself teaching "according to the sim,"
tell us — that means the *sim* is wrong, not your method. We change the sim to fit you, never the
reverse.

## What we ask you to send — two recordings

### 1. A real class — you + a student, screen-recorded

- Teach the concept the way you normally would, with the sim open as your visual.
- **Teach naturally — don't perform for the camera, and don't explain the sim itself.** Just teach the
  student.
- Let the student ask questions — that's the most valuable part. We watch where the student gets
  confused, and where *you* had to step away from the sim to explain something. Every such moment tells
  us what the sim is missing.
- You do **not** need to pretend to be a student or guess "will a student understand this?" — your real
  student answers that for us.

### 2. Your per-state review — the 7 questions

Go through the sim **state by state**, and for each state answer the seven questions below. Deliver this
whichever way is easier for you:

- **record yourself** narrating the 6 answers per state as you click through, **or**
- **fill the Excel template** → [`templates/per_state_review.csv`](templates/per_state_review.csv).

**The 7 questions (per state):**

1. **Correct?** — Is the physics in this state right?
2. **Right order?** — Does this state build on the one before it? Is anything shown or named *before* the
   state that teaches it? (Foundation first.)
3. **Labels & arrows?** — Are the symbols, direction arrows, and equations you need on screen, where a
   direction or a quantity is being taught?
4. **Reads with sound OFF?** — Mute it. Does the picture alone carry the point? (We default to no audio
   in class.)
5. **Clearly different?** — Did this state feel clearly different from the previous one — and worth its
   own click? (If two states blur together, tell us which.)
6. **How would you use it?** — Where would you point? Would you skip this state? How does it fit your
   explanation?
7. **Anything missing, wrong, or confusing?** — The one thing you'd fix.

**Also — your recommended state order.** Tell us the order *you* would teach these states in (e.g.
"I'd swap 3 and 4"). Your recommended flow becomes the sim's default order.

## A few practicalities

- **Keep each pass small — about 5 changes.** A focused list beats an exhaustive one; we iterate in
  rounds.
- **Sending files:** drop both recordings (and the Excel, if you filled it) into the shared Google Drive
  folder. Name them `<concept> <date>` — e.g. `electric field 18 Jun`.
- **The spoken narration:** by default we keep the sim's voice **off** — you narrate in your own voice.
  If we ever ask you to also check the narration *text*, it'll be a separate, clearly-labeled pass
  ("audit this for correctness — you will NOT teach from it"). Keep it separate from "is the sim right?"
- **Privacy:** the class recording includes your student — please make sure they (or a parent) are okay
  being recorded for our product review. The Drive folder is private to you and us.
