1. What we are building, and your role

PhysicsMind builds interactive physics simulations that teach exactly along the Indian curriculum (NCERT / JEE / NEET). Every simulation is a sequence of states — visual steps that build one concept from zero to exam-ready.

Your job: test each simulation like a teacher and a former student at the same time. Find what is unclear, too fast, too crowded, missing, or wrong — state by state — so we can fix it. Your feedback directly changes the product. This is paid, professional review work.

3. The Golden Rules (read these twice)

  1. NEVER open a simulation before your First-Look session. Your honest first impression is the most valuable data we collect, and it can only happen once. If you preview the sim, the Packet cannot be accepted. You will always receive the link only at the start of the session.
  2. Every state gets a verdict. Good / Minor issue / Major issue. “This state is good, here is why it works” is equally paid work — you are not paid more for finding more problems.
  3. Be specific. Every flag must say WHAT exactly, WHERE exactly, and WHY. See examples in Section 6.
  4. Every flag points to a timestamp in your recording (mm:ss), so we can see the exact moment you mean.
  5. Work independently. Do not discuss a simulation with any other reviewer before you submit your sheet.
  6. Honesty over politeness. We are not looking for compliments. A defect you find today is a defect no student ever sees.

4. Step-by-step: completing one Packet

Step 1 — Setup. Join the scheduled Zoom call (for your first simulations, the founder hosts and gives you remote control). Recording is ON from the start — the recording is part of your deliverable.

Step 2 — First-Look (Part A). The simulation link is shared NOW, not before. Watch it in auto mode with narration ON, first state to last. Speak every thought aloud as it comes: “this is clear” · “wait, why did the arrow flip?” · “this text is too small” · “this moved too fast for a student.” You may pause and replay. The founder stays silent — this is your moment, not a discussion.

Step 3 — Teach it (Part B). Now mute the narration. You are the teacher. Teach the concept your own way, using the simulation as your blackboard: jump between states in any order, replay animations, move the sliders. Your “student” will ask questions — answer as you would in a real class.

Step 4 — Fill the Review Sheet (Part C). Open the Google Sheet tab for this simulation (one row per state). For every state choose a verdict: Good (works — one line on why) · Minor (works, should improve — reason + fix required) · Major (confusing, wrong, or broken — reason + fix required). Pick the category (too fast / too crowded / missing visual / wrong emphasis / physics error / confusing narration / other) and add the recording timestamp. Finish the same day or the next day, while memory is fresh.

Step 5 — Submit. WhatsApp: “Packet ready: [simulation name]”. Checklist first:

  • First-Look recording exists and has audio
  • Teaching recording exists and has audio
  • Every state row has a verdict
  • Every Minor/Major has a specific reason AND a suggested fix
  • Timestamps filled in

The founder checks the Packet against the recordings within 1–2 days and releases payment.

Simulation opened before First-LookWait for the session — always

A rejected part can usually be fixed and resubmitted once — we will tell you exactly what is missing.

6. What a good flag looks like

❌ Not acceptable✅ Acceptable
“State 4 is confusing.”“State 4: the field arrows appear at the same moment the narration introduces the formula — a student doesn’t know whether to read or watch. Suggest: show arrows first, bring the formula 3–4 seconds later. (Recording 12:40)”
“Too much text.”“State 2: three labels overlap near the wire on the right side; on a projector they would be unreadable. Suggest: move the current label above the wire. (06:15)”
“Students won’t get this.”“State 6: the right-hand rule is shown but the thumb direction is never connected to conventional current — my students always confuse this. Suggest: one extra beat highlighting thumb = current. (21:05)”
