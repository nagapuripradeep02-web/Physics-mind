## 1. Atomic claim

This concept teaches ONE decision-shaped idea: **when you join real cells (each ε, r), series ADDS both the pushes and the tolls (ε_eq = Σεᵢ signed by polarity, r_eq = Σrᵢ always), parallel keeps the push but SHARES the toll (identical cells: ε_eq = ε, r_eq = r/m) — so the right grouping depends on the load: series wins when R ≫ r, parallel wins when R ≲ r, and "more cells" is never automatically "more current."** It does NOT cover: maximum power transfer or mixed m×n grouping optimization (deferred to `grouping_cells_mixed_max_power_transfer`, A20 — cited as a pointer only), emf's definition or single-cell V = ε − ir mechanics (shipped `emf_definition` / `internal_resistance` — ASSUMED here), Kirchhoff's laws as a method (shipped KCL/KVL — loop reasoning is used informally, never re-taught), or the general UNEQUAL-cell parallel formula ε_eq = (ε₁r₂ + ε₂r₁)/(r₁ + r₂) (deferred to drill-down — see SCOPE note below).

**One-diamond decision (A14 + A15 together, not split):** the PRIMARY aha — *choosing* series vs parallel against the load — requires BOTH groupings live in one apparatus; splitting into two diamonds would orphan the aha in whichever half lacks the other grouping. Exact precedent: `combination_of_resistors` shipped series + parallel as one 9-state diamond for the same reason. 8 states sits inside the §5 complex band (7–9); not an escalation case.

**SCOPE note — unequal parallel cells (my call, with reasoning):** NCERT §3.12 does derive the two-unequal-cell parallel result via loop reasoning, but it is DEFERRED here to a named drill-down cluster, not a guided state, because (i) it is Kirchhoff bookkeeping that adds NO new motion archetype — the conceptual payload of parallel grouping (ε unchanged, internal toll shared) is fully carried by the identical-cell case; (ii) board/exam emphasis is the identical-cell results plus the series signed sum; (iii) unequal parallel cells introduce inter-cell circulating currents — a genuinely separate confusion that deserves its own reactive surface (`unequal_parallel_cells` cluster, §6), not 55 words of guided time; (iv) the guided arc's aha does not need it. The identical-cell restriction is stated honestly on-canvas at S6 ("identical cells" rider on the formula surface).

**SCOPE note — reversed cell (my call):** IN as a guided state (S4). It is genuine NCERT §3.12 scope (signed EMF sum), it is a documented exam trap in BOTH directions (students either ignore polarity and write 3 V, or think a cancelled pair also cancels its resistance — r_eq is still Σr), and it buys the arc its `rotate/flip` archetype with the most dramatic cheap visual in the concept: flip one cell → the circuit drops dead at zero.

## 9. Real-world anchor (Rule 35 — universal, culture-neutral)

**Primary:** a **flashlight** stacks its cells nose-to-tail — in series — because its lamp wants more push than one 1.5 V cell can give: two cells, three volts, one brighter beam. Put one cell in backwards and the flashlight goes completely dead even though both cells are full — the reversed cell subtracts its push (S4, literally). But series stacking is not what a **cordless drill or a car's starter** needs: a motor straining against a load is a tiny-resistance, huge-current problem, and there the pack's own internal resistance is the bottleneck — packs built for heavy current put cells (or cell strings) side-by-side so each one carries only a share and the pack doesn't sag. Same cells, two opposite architectures, chosen by the load. This hooks a Class 12 student because both objects are in their daily reach, and the S4 flip explains a failure every one of them has personally caused with a torch or a remote control.
**Secondary (one line, S6/S7 territory):** battery holders in toys and remotes are shaped to FORCE the right grouping — the springs and nubs are the manufacturer's answer to exactly this concept.
